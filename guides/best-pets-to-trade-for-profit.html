Best Pets to Trade for Profit in Grow a Garden

Not all pets are worth the same in a trade. Some hold their value better, some spike after updates, and some are just hard to get rid of. Here is what actually moves in the market right now.

Why some pets trade better than others

Value is not just about the number on a list. A pet that shows 500K on TradePulse might sit in your inventory for weeks if nobody wants it. Demand matters just as much as the listed price. Pets that are rare, useful in mutations, or popular in the community tend to move fast. The trick is knowing which ones those are.

High-demand pets right now

Based on recent trade activity on TradePulse, these pets are seeing the most offers:

  • Celestial Dragon (Divine) - 425K-575K. Stable demand, rarely drops.
  • Divine Prism (Divine) - 380K-520K. Always wanted for mutation breeding.
  • Cosmic Lotus (Divine) - 350K-480K. Popular with collectors.
  • Chaos Prism (Divine) - 300K-440K. Good entry point for divine-tier trading.
  • Eternal Prism (Divine) - 280K-400K. Steady trades, not flashy but reliable.

Divine-tier pets dominate the high-demand list. That is not an accident.

Best pets for beginners

If you are just starting out, you probably do not have a Divine pet yet. There is a whole market below the top tier:

  • Eggsnapper (Mythic) - Easy to find, steady demand from new players.
  • Dimensional Fox (Mythic) - Good looks + decent stats = consistent trades.
  • Cosmos Dragon (Mythic) - Pairs well in mutations, keeps its value.

Start with mid-tier pets, trade up gradually. That is how most active traders built their inventory.

Quick tip: Check the TradePulse value list before accepting any trade. Values shift after game updates and some pets lose 20-30% overnight.

Pets to avoid

Some pets look valuable on paper but are tough to move:

  • Event pets from old seasons (high supply, low demand)
  • Pets with unclear rarity tags (hard to price)
  • Anything that dropped in value more than twice in the last month

How to spot a good trade

  1. Look up each pet on TradePulse values
  2. Compare the total value of both sides
  3. Check demand — is the pet you are getting actually wanted?
  4. Think about mutation potential
  5. Post the trade for community votes if unsure
